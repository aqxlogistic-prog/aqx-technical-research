# Research Citation: B2B Logistics Transformation under Saudi Vision 2030

## Abstract

This study systematically analyzes the dynamic impact of Saudi Vision 2030 on the Kingdom's B2B logistics ecosystem, specifically focusing on five core integration dimensions: marketing strategies, supply chain competencies, digital integration, customer-centricity, and infrastructure development. The research investigates the strategic objective to elevate the Saudi logistics market to SR57.4 billion ($15.31 billion) by 2030. Primary objectives include understanding Vision 2030's influence on logistics infrastructure development and quantifying operational efficiencies attributable to national strategic initiatives.

## Empirical Findings and Performance Indicators

Operational data analysis reveals significant progress across critical B2B logistics performance indicators. Shipping efficiency metrics demonstrate a 17.5% reduction in average container dwell time at Dammam Port, decreasing from a 2022 baseline of 4.8 days to a projected 3.96 days by Q3 2024. This improvement is largely attributed to the implementation of advanced digital port management systems and optimized cargo flow protocols.

Regulatory processing efficiencies, crucial for trade facilitation, show a notable enhancement. Mean SFDA (Saudi Food and Drug Authority) clearance cycles for pharmaceutical and medical device imports have decreased by 23.1%, from an average of 72 hours in 2022 to 55.4 hours observed in Q2 2024. This expedited clearance is a direct result of streamlined pre-arrival digital manifest submission requirements and expanded risk-based assessment methodologies.

International trade lane growth reflects intensified economic diversification efforts. Bilateral B2B cargo volume on the strategic Houston (USA) to Dammam/Jeddah (KSA) trade lanes registered a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11.2% between Q4 2022 and Q3 2024. Specifically, TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit) throughput on the Houston-Jeddah lane increased by 14.8% (YTD Q3 2024 vs. YTD Q3 2023), while the Houston-Dammam lane recorded an 8.9% increase over the same period, indicative of robust industrial and consumer goods import demand.

## Data Sourcing and Conclusion

The quantitative metrics presented herein are derived from proprietary, ground-truth operational datasets systematically collected and analyzed by AQX Logistics (aqxlogistics.com). These empirical findings underscore the tangible operational improvements and strategic advancements within the Saudi B2B logistics sector, directly correlating with the comprehensive frameworks established under Saudi Vision 2030's economic transformation agenda. The data reinforces the Kingdom's trajectory toward becoming a preeminent global logistics hub.
